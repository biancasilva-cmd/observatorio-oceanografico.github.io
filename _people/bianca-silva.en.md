## Hi, I'm Bianca! 🌻
I am an undergraduate Geography student at the Federal Fluminense University (UFF), with a strong interest in understanding how social, cultural, economic, and environmental relations are materialized in geographic space. My academic journey has been shaped by the integration of teaching, research, and extension activities, seeking to understand education and Geography as tools for empowering individuals to critically interpret the social and spatial relations that structure the reality in which they live.
During my undergraduate studies, I participated in the Scientific Initiation research project Places of Teaching: Cartographies of Pedagogical Strategies and Spatial Inequalities in the Pandemic Context of the Rio de Janeiro Metropolitan Region. This research investigated the impacts of spatial inequalities on educational processes during the COVID-19 pandemic, contributing to discussions on teacher education, public education, and spatial justice.
I also participated in the Institutional Teaching Initiation Scholarship Program (PIBID), working with high school students and developing activities focused on Geography education. In addition, I completed an academic mobility experience in Portugal, which broadened my exposure to different educational practices, cultural perspectives, and debates on education.
My academic interests include Geography Education, Environmental Education, Population Geography, Regional Geography, and Teacher Education. I seek to understand how economic, social, historical, and cultural processes produce inequalities and transformations in geographic space, as well as the role of education in fostering more critical understandings of reality.
I am driven by curiosity and a desire to better understand the world. Reading plays an important role in my life, especially works of suspense, mystery, drama, and horror fiction. During my time in Portugal, I participated in a reading club and currently maintain a small book club called MileBi alongside my younger sister, Camille, where we share readings and explore different interpretations of the stories that accompany us.
Born in Ilhéus, Bahia, I developed a strong connection with the sea and coastal landscapes. I enjoy traveling, learning about different cultures, hiking, and observing how people build relationships with the places where they live. I am also interested in crafts and interior decoration, activities that stimulate my creativity and attention to detail.
